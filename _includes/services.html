What We Do

We are scientific computing study group whose home-base is at Simon Fraser University (SFU) in Burnaby, BC, Canada. Our group meets on a weekly basis for peer-facilitated study-sessions on topics under the broad umbrella of using computers to do scientific research more effectively and efficiently. This includes using Python for DNA sequence analysis, plotting and statistical analysis of environmental data in R, automating repetitive tasks with the shell, general best practices in coding, using tools such as Git and Github to collaborate effectively with others, and much much more.

All are welcome to this study group, regardless of scientific research area, affiliation or training level.

Skill Sharing

Co-Working

Get together to work on your coding projects, help each other out and share your work.
